You can avoid would-be scammers by following these common-sense rules:

  • Deal locally with folks you can meet in person, and you will avoid 99% of scam attempts.
  • Never wire funds using Western Union, MoneyGram, or and other wire service, because anyone who asks you to do so is likely a scammer.
  • Fake cashier checks and money orders are very common, and banks will hold you responsible when the fraud is discovered weeks later.
  • We are not involved in any transaction, and does not handle payments, provide escrow, "buyer protection" or "seller certification."
  • Never give out financial information (bank account number, social security number, eBay/PayPal info, etc.).
  • Avoid deals involving and shipping or escrow service and know that only a scammer will "guarantee" your transaction.
  • Do not receive housing, goods, or donations without seeing them first, because the may not exist or be in poor condition.
  • Do not submit to credit or background check until you have met the volunteer coordinator in person.
  • Do not respond to email solicitations.
  • Delete unsolicited emails and any with attachments.
  • Research any group that ask for donations through social media.
  • Don't donate anything to people who contact you online claiming to be a victim of a natural disaster.
  • Give through a reputable and secure service.
  • Give any donations directly to a charity and not through a middle man.

Who should I notify about fraud or scam attempts?

  • Internet Fraud Complaint Center
  • FTC Video: How to report scams to the FTC
  • FTC online complaint form
  • FTC toll free hotline: 877-FTC-HELP (877-382-4357)
  • Consumer Sentinel/Military (for reports from services members or their families)
  • SIIA Software and Content Piracy reporting

If you are defrauded by someone you met in person, contact your local police department.

Most scams involve one or more of the following:

  • Inquiry from someone far away, often in another country.
  • Western Union, Money Gram, cashier's check, money order, shipping, escrow service, or a "guarantee."
  • Inability or refusal to meet face-to-face before consumating transaction.

Examples of Scams.

  1. Someone claims craigslist will guarantee a transaction, certify a donor/charity, OR will handle or provide protection for a payment.
  2. Distant person offers a genuine-looking (but fake) cashier's check.
  3. Someone requests wire service payment via Western Union or MoneyGram.
  4. Distant person offers to send you a money order and then have you wire money.
  5. Distant person suggests use of an online escrow service.
  6. Distant person asks for a partial payment upfront, after which he will ship goods.
  7. Foreign company offers you a job receiving payments from customers, then wiring funds.
